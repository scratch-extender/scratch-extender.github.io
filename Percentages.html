Percentages

Percent is made of 2 words, per and cent. Per means per or every, cent is the Latin word for hundred. So percent literally means per hundred or every hundred. They are really useful in :-

  • Finance
    1. Loans
    2. Interest rates
    3. FDs
    4. Cost projections
    5. And much more
  • Data collection
    1. Marks
    2. Attendance
    3. Advertisement
    4. Change in biodiversity
    5. And much more

Percentages can be used instead of fractions. 4/5 = 80 % (% is the symbol of percentage), so instead of saying four fifths of the muffins are sold, you say 80 % of the muffins are sold. There are many ways to turn fractions into percentages, one of the easiest is multiplying the fraction by 100. For example:-
(4 / 5) * 100 = 400 / 5 = 80, so 80 %.
(3 / 20) * 100 = 300 / 20 = 15, so 15 %.
(7 / 9) * 100 = 700 / 9 = (63 + 7/9), so (63 + 7/9)%
1 * 100 = 100 %
1.35 * 100 = 135%
To turn a percentage into a fraction, you do not need to do much. Simply attach an upon 100 and the simplify. For example:-
70 % = 70 / 100 = 7 / 10
63 % = 63 / 100
And so on.
You can also find some percent of a number. You do this by first turning the percentage to a fraction, then multiplying the fraction and whole number. For example :-
50 % of 30 = (1 / 2) * 30 = 15
37 % of 300 = (37 / 100) * 300 = 111

Write the percentage as a fraction, then simplify (simplification is compulsory), if it is a whole number, write it as the percentage in the numerator and 1 in the denominator.
